    Castle Wars

    Castle Wars is a vanilla mini-game map I build in Minecraft. The idea originally came from a mini-game called Bed Wars which is on most popular Minecraft server one way or another. The concept is simple. There are two teams, the objective is to collect resources, trade them for materials and weapons, and cross the map to the other team castle and destroy their beacon. Players have infinite lives while the beacon isn't destroy, but if the team's beacon is destroy that team is on their last lives. Team left alive at the end wins.

    One of the difficulty of the map is making the generator room. In this room, player would be able to obtain ore gems from the blocks on the ground and they would be able to go to the trader room to use them for valuable items. These generators spawn in iron ingots, gold ingots, or diamonds at a specific rate, depended on the level of the generator. Each generator at the beginning of the game is set to level 1, or level 0 and it up to the player to upgrade them based on the requirement stated on the sign. They can be upgraded to the max level which is level 4.

    In addition to the generators in each castle, their are 5 islands outside of both castles with diamond and emerald generators, each ores are offered to trade stronger gear back in the trade room so for players to be better equiped. They must build out to these islands. In fact, in order for diamonds to spawn in the castle, you need diamonds to get the generator to level 1, so it required for players to build out in order to gain these ores. These generators upgrade depended on time, every 5 minutes the diamond or emerald generators upgrade to the next level, to a max of 4 levels.

    The trader room is filled with the villager mob from vanilla Minecraft, but what makes them different is the custom trades they offer. Each villager theme is based on the items they give, and when you hover over the villager with your crossheir they are named for the items they are trading for. The different villager offer blocks, weapons, tools, food, enchantments, and even potions. This was possible as Minecraft allow users to edit almost all aspect of a mob. With the villager since they are the only mob that can trade, we can edit that part using a specific command. Each mob have their own predicates and parameters. Villagers have trades, slimes have size, you can even apply armor to zombies and skeletons. You can even turn off the AI of any mob in the game. These are just a few examples how Minecraft allows users to custom their game experience and allows people to make maps like this.

    One of the unique aspects to my map, is a room where you can send over mobs to the other team side to fight the enemy team. This can help distract the team or even end up killing them, setting them back a few paces. In the trader room there a villager that sell certain items which can be used on these traders to get spawn eggs. These spawn eggs range in difficulty of mob and the cost is appropriate for the strength of the mob. Stronger mobs cost more. Once you buy your spawn eggs you can drop them into hoppers at the end of the room, and they will be send over to the other team side. Once they reach the other team they will attack the other team and it up to the other team to defeat their distraction.

    This is what I called the Beacon of Life. This is sitting in the throne in the back of the castle. The team has to protect their beacon anyway possible, this includes, putting stronger blocks around the beacon to make it harder for the other team to break it. Teams have to find ways of breaking into the beacon throne and try to break before times is up. However if teams are too slow the beacon will break within time leaving all teams on their last life.

    Everytime I build a map, I always try to use a new feature Minecraft adds to help map builder make complicated maps. This feature is called Data Packs. These Data Packs are able to let players run in-game commands as the world runs. This feature allows players to add in new advancements, dimensions, function, loop tables, predicates, crafting recipes, structures, tags, world generation modifications, and biomes, without any coding modifications. Minecraft commands are in a way a coding language with each commands acting as a normal code statement. I use the data pack to assist with the generators, all of them (You can see key words like IronGenerator etc.). The way the generator works is that if I spawn in a armor stand with a specific tag, the data pack will detect it and depended on the level the armor stand is set to, will spawn the ore at a rate using scoreboard commands as a timer. However I needed to use in-map command blocks to help with upgrading the generators. This honestly is a simple data pack, as it my first. I am currently working on upgrading the data pack so I can spawn it with a simple spawn egg and have the upgrade feature go a little more smoothy without in-map command blocks assisting the upgrading procress.






    Five Nights at Freddy Map Remake

    Five Nights at Freddy's Map Remake has been an ongoing project I been working on since the series had came out. This project has span from multiple tries of making the map, each time making the map I use better techniques to complete my goals. This map project uses many commands blocks and a resource pack that I made from stretch. The map was original started as a multiplayer remake map where players became the animatronics and attack the guard. Since then, I made a better resource pack, updated textures and adding sounds effects from the actually game. With this recent redo of the map I had added AI so that the animatronics can still remain active even if a player isn't in control, and this includes a hardcore mode, where the AI moves much faster and less unpredictable.

    Once you exit the main hub, you head into this room where you can customize the night, this specific side of the room includes the character select. The options being the Night Guard, Freddy, Bonnie, Chica, and Foxy. It important that their is always a Guard, as the Guard starts the night when players are ready. Other players can select one of the animatronics and they will get armor and be teleported into the map when the game starts. The objective of the guard is to survive until 6am (about 9 minutes in real life time, as long as the original game) and the objective of the animatronic is to end the night guard before 6am. If no one picks an animatronic it will be replace by an AI armor stand and move on it own throughout the night. If players don't pick an animatronic they will be put into spectator mode, where they can teleport freely and can't interact with the map. Just as a side note, in this room also includes the Phone Call, which I voice acted for, which acts like a tutorial.

    On the other side of the character select is the AI side where players can customize the AI of the animatronics. On the right side, you have the option of turning off Freddy, Bonnie, Chica, Foxy, and a bonus character Golden Freddy AI. Turning off the AI won't effect anything if the players picks the character, as when a player chooses an animatronic the AI is automatically turned off. On the left side, is the button that activates hardcore mode, which makes the Freddy, Bonnie, and Chica more active and unpredictable than a standard night. As the creator of the map, it took me a good 8 attempts before beating it for the first time. It definitly gives the map a much needed difficulty boost. Hardcore mode can be activated while players are animatronic, however it will not effect any player timers and cooldowns as humans are already as unpredicable as it stands.

    This is the office where the night guard is. Inside, like in the original game, includes a poster of the main animatronics, some children drawings and a course two blast doors and lights, with companying buttons. Let not forget the most dangerous object in the room, the fan. The guard also equiped with a camera, where they can use to see the different areas of the map and keep taps on the animatronics locations. Throughout the night every players knows the power left in the office, it determined by the guard usage of doors, lights, and the cameras, and what time it currently is, represented by the green number above the hub bar.

    When the night starts, the animatronics begin off at the Show Stage, the Guard can view the Show Stage using their camera. Also as a note when the night starts and every time an interval of time passes titles will appear announcing to all players that it the next hour. But as the animatronics you also get a camera which tell you where you can teleport next, moving you closer to the office. AI just run through the map normally, but players have the option of being decisive when they choose to move or attack.

    When the night goes on, animatronics will reach the office and it your job to keep them out, before they get into your office and end your night. Bonnie and Chica appear outside the office (like in the picture) before they attack the guard. One cool feature I added in this recent verison is that the animatronic will appear invisible when they get to the office door, unless you use the lights to view them, making it mandatory to use the lights to tell whether the animatronic is in front of the office. However, it is noted that Freddy, works different and doesn't appear in front of the door before he attacks (like he does in the original game) and instead attack straight from CAM 4B.

    The fourth animatronic, is Foxy and he works differently then the others. He starts off in Pirate's Cove (CAM 1C) and if you don't watch him on your camera, he will slowly leave the cove. If you neglect Foxy long enough he will begin to run down the hallway right into your office. New feature of the map, is that if Foxy's AI is on, an animation of him running down the hallway can be seen. If you react fast enough Foxy will bang on your door and steal a random amount of power, but if you are too slow you will be greeted with a jumpscare. This is why Foxy is incredible important to always keep watch of him, to prevent him from slipping away and possibly messing with your night. I'll also mention if you lose power in your office, you won't be able to use the doors, lights, nor your camera. A sequence will play where Freddy play a tune for you (like in the game), length of the tune is randomize, and if you make it to 6am you win, but if you don't Freddy will end your night.

    When you press the red or white button you will turn on that sides respective door or light. Which each second you have your doors or lights on will drain your power more. So it recommended to only use them wisely, say an animatronic is outside your office. These are your only defense and messing up once will cost you your night, and you will be taken to the game over screen. But don't worry no animatronic can break down the door and they are 100% effective, that is unless you run out of power.

    In the original game, Golden Freddy is only an easter egg, however in my map he a new threat and can be very annoying if you see him at the wrong time. Only as an AI, Golden Freddy appears on CAM 2B, and can pull you from your camera into the office and if you don't use your camera again, he will end your night.

    This is the full map, showing all the command blocks. Command Blocks act as a single statement of code for the map. Each one help make this map possible, from the AI, starting the night, ending the night, and a course reseting the map. For the first game I'm surprised how few command blocks I used but compared to the previous FNAF maps I tried I definitly saw the procress I made with my spacing and usage of command blocks.
